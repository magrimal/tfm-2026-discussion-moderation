Core Principles of AI Ethics

This unit introduces the major ethical frameworks applied to artificial intelligence systems. We focus on four principles that appear consistently across policy documents and academic literature:

  • Beneficence: AI systems should produce benefits for individuals and society.
  • Non-maleficence: AI systems should not cause harm, directly or indirectly.
  • Autonomy: people should retain meaningful control over decisions that affect them.
  • Justice: the benefits and harms of AI should be distributed fairly.

These principles often conflict. A system that maximizes benefit for a majority may harm a minority (beneficence vs. justice). A system that protects autonomy may refuse to act in time-sensitive situations (autonomy vs. beneficence).
