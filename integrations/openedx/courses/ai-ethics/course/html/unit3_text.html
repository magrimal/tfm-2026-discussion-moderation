Governance Frameworks and Accountability

Principles and fairness criteria are necessary but not sufficient. Without governance structures, they remain aspirational. This unit examines how accountability is built into AI systems at an organizational and regulatory level.

Regulatory approaches

  • EU AI Act: risk-based regulation; high-risk systems require conformity assessments and human oversight mechanisms.
  • Algorithmic accountability bills: require impact assessments for automated decision systems in public services.
  • Sector-specific rules: financial services (ECOA, fair lending) and healthcare (FDA guidance on AI/ML devices).

Organizational mechanisms

  • AI ethics boards (advisory vs. binding authority).
  • Model cards and datasheets for transparency.
  • Audit and red-teaming processes.
  • Procurement standards and vendor accountability clauses.
